About Gandalf the Grey

In J.R.R. Tolkien's legendarium, Gandalf's full name is Olórin. However, Gandalf is commonly known by several other names, each reflecting different aspects of his character and role in Middle-earth. Here are some of his notable names:

Olórin: This is Gandalf's original name as a Maia spirit. Maia is a type of divine spirit in Tolkien's mythology, and Olórin was one of the Maiar sent by the Valar (higher divine beings) to help guide the Free Peoples of Middle-earth.

Gandalf: This is the name by which he is most commonly known in Middle-earth. It's a name of the North, likely of Norse origin, and it means "Elf of the Wand" or "Elf of the Staff." The name reflects his association with wizards and his use of a staff as a symbol of his power.

Mithrandir: This is the name given to Gandalf by the elves. It means "Grey Pilgrim" or "Grey Wanderer," referring to his grey robes and his extensive travels throughout Middle-earth.

Tharkûn: This is a name used for Gandalf by the Dwarves. It means "Staff-man" or "Man of the Staff," emphasizing his use of a staff and his association with magical powers.

Gandalf's origin lies in the spiritual realm as a Maia, and he is essentially a divine being sent to help guide and protect Middle-earth from the threat of the Dark Lord, Sauron. Gandalf plays a crucial role in guiding the Fellowship of the Ring, providing wisdom, and actively participating in the battle against Sauron. His leadership and magical abilities significantly impact the story's outcome.

Timeline of Gandalf the Grey's Life

Before Time Immemorial: Olórin, a Maia spirit, is chosen by the Valar to be sent to Middle-earth.
Beginning of the Third Age: Olórin takes on a human-like form and becomes known as Gandalf. He joins the order of the Istari, a group of wizards sent to guide the Free Peoples of Middle-earth against the rising darkness.
Approx. 1000 - 1050 of the Third Age: Gandalf, along with the other wizards, arrives in Middle-earth to fulfill his mission.
Approx. 2063 of the Third Age: Gandalf discovers the One Ring's true identity while investigating the necromancer in Dol Guldur.
Approx. 2850 of the Third Age: Gandalf actively opposes Sauron's return in the guise of the Necromancer, aiding in the White Council's efforts to drive him out of Dol Guldur.
2941 of the Third Age: Gandalf plays a crucial role in the events of "The Hobbit," guiding Bilbo Baggins and the dwarves on their quest to reclaim Erebor.
Approx. 3018 - 3019 of the Third Age: Gandalf serves as a key member of the Fellowship of the Ring. He aids in the quest to destroy the One Ring and battles against various challenges, including the Balrog in Moria.
Approx. 3021 of the Third Age: Gandalf's mission in Middle-earth is fulfilled, and he departs with the Elves across the sea.
The Fourth Age: Gandalf is believed to have returned to the Undying Lands.
